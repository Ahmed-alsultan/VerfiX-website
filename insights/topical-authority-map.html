Strategy Overview

How Topical Authority Works for VerfiX

Topical authority is built when a website covers an entire subject area comprehensively — not just a few pages, but every meaningful sub-topic, question, and angle. Google's algorithms increasingly reward websites that demonstrate deep expertise in a domain over those that produce isolated, unconnected content.

For VerfiX, the domain is identity verification, KYC, AML and digital identity compliance — a specialised, high-intent B2B topic with significant search volume from compliance officers, developers, and decision-makers at financial institutions. Building authority here means covering every article on this map, linking them into clusters, and ensuring all cluster content links back to the relevant pillar page.

The 100 articles below are organised into 7 topic clusters, each anchored by an existing pillar page. Every article has a defined primary keyword, search intent (informational, commercial, or transactional), content difficulty level, and specific internal links to related articles in the cluster and adjacent clusters.
