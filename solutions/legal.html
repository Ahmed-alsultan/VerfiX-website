Industry Challenges

Law firms face growing AML obligations — with limited compliance infrastructure

Legal professionals subject to GwG are held to the same standard as banks but typically lack the compliance teams and technology infrastructure to meet those obligations efficiently.

GwG Article 2(3) obligations for legal professionals

Swiss law firms and legal professionals acting as financial intermediaries — advising on transactions, managing client assets, establishing companies on behalf of clients — are subject to GwG AML obligations including client identification, beneficial owner identification, SECO screening, and ten-year documentation retention.

Complex client entity structures

Legal clients frequently operate through complex entity structures — multi-jurisdiction holding companies, foundations, discretionary trusts, family offices. Tracing beneficial ownership through these structures is time-intensive and a primary gap in law firm AML programmes.

International clients from high-risk jurisdictions

Law firms advising on cross-border transactions serve clients from FATF-listed high-risk jurisdictions. These engagements require Enhanced Due Diligence — source of funds documentation, enhanced ongoing monitoring, and senior partner sign-off.

Client onboarding speed vs compliance completeness

Fee-earning partners resist compliance processes that delay client onboarding. Manual KYC — ID document collection, registry checks, AML screening — creates friction that conflicts with client service expectations and causes compliance shortcuts.

Ongoing monitoring across the client lifecycle

GwG obligations are not limited to onboarding. Sanctions lists change daily. A client clean at engagement inception may be listed during the matter. Ongoing monitoring across the full client book is an obligation few law firms fulfil systematically.

FINMA and cantonal supervisory scrutiny

Swiss bar associations and FINMA-licensed financial intermediary organisations are increasing their AML compliance programme requirements for member law firms. Failure to demonstrate systematic KYC and AML processes creates disciplinary risk.

The VerfiX Approach

How VerfiX brings banking-grade AML to legal practice

VerfiX automates the AML compliance obligations that take law firms hours of manual work per client — without disrupting the client relationship.

Digital client identification — no document scanning

Clients complete identity verification from their own device in under three minutes. The lawyer sends a secure verification link; the client photographs their identity document and takes a selfie; VerfiX returns a structured identity record and AML clearance. No document scanning, no emailing PDFs, no manual data entry.

Entity client verification and beneficial owner mapping

For corporate and institutional clients, VerfiX queries the relevant company registry — Zefix for Swiss entities, major EU registries for European clients — maps the ownership structure, and identifies all UBOs above the GwG 25% threshold. All identified UBOs are routed through the individual KYC flow. The complete KYB audit trail is generated without manual research.

SECO and global AML screening — immediate results

Client onboarding triggers simultaneous screening against SECO, UN, OFAC, EU, and PEP databases. Results are returned within seconds — not the day or two required for manual checks. PEP matches trigger a structured EDD workflow that prompts collection of source of funds documentation.

Ongoing monitoring across the full client book

Every active client is automatically rescreened against updated sanctions and PEP lists at a configurable frequency — daily for high-risk clients, weekly for standard. New matches trigger webhook alerts to the responsible partner or compliance officer. All rescreening events are logged in the audit trail.

Matter management system integration

VerfiX integrates with legal matter management systems via REST API. KYC completion status and AML clearance are visible within the existing matter management workflow — not in a separate compliance portal. New matter intake can be configured to require KYC completion before file opening.

Supervisory-inspection-ready client files

The complete GwG documentation package for any client — identification records, AML screening history, ongoing monitoring logs, EDD documentation — is exportable in structured format for bar association or cantonal supervisory authority inspection within one hour.

Verification Workflow

From request to verified result in seconds

VerfiX slots into your existing stack. No rearchitecting. One REST API call returns document data, biometric result, AML screening outcome, and a FINMA-compliant audit record.

01

New matter intake — KYC triggered

Fee earner opens a new matter. The matter management system triggers a VerfiX KYC session for each new client. A secure verification link is sent to the client.

02

Client completes digital verification remotely

Client photographs their identity document and takes a selfie with liveness check from their own device. Completion takes under three minutes. No in-person meeting required for standard matters.

03

Entity clients — registry lookup and UBO identification

For corporate clients, VerfiX queries the relevant registry, maps the ownership chain, identifies UBOs, and routes them through the KYC flow. The KYB audit trail is generated without partner input.

04

AML screening and risk assessment

Verified client identity is screened against SECO, UN, OFAC, EU, and PEP databases. Trust Score calculated. Standard clients receive clearance. PEPs and high-risk jurisdiction clients trigger EDD workflow.

05

Matter file opens — ongoing monitoring begins

KYC-complete clients have their matter file opened. Ongoing monitoring begins automatically — clients rescreened at scheduled intervals throughout the matter lifecycle. New matches alert the responsible partner.

FAQ

Common questions

Swiss law firms and individual legal professionals are subject to GwG obligations when they act as financial intermediaries under GwG Article 2(3). This includes: managing client assets; holding client funds; acting as company director or corporate officer for a client; establishing, managing, or administering legal entities on behalf of clients; and advising on the buying and selling of businesses above GwG thresholds. Legal advice alone — without asset management or transaction intermediary functions — is generally outside GwG scope. Individual assessment with GwG counsel is recommended.

VerfiX integrates via REST API with any system capable of making HTTPS requests. We have documentation for common legal matter management platforms. The integration returns KYC completion status and AML clearance data that can trigger matter opening workflows. Webhook notifications enable real-time status updates within the matter management system.

VerfiX processes only identity verification data — document images, selfie, and the resulting verification record. Privileged communications, matter details, and legal strategy are never processed or transmitted through VerfiX. Identity verification data (name, date of birth, document number) is not privileged information. The VerfiX DPA confirms that verification data is processed solely for the identity verification purpose with no secondary processing.

For existing clients being onboarded onto VerfiX ongoing monitoring, the firm submits client identity records through the batch onboarding API. VerfiX verifies data completeness, begins rescreening on the next scheduled cycle, and alerts on any matches. Clients with incomplete historical KYC records are flagged for refresh — a process that can be prioritised by client risk tier.
