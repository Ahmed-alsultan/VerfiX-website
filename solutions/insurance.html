Industry Challenges

Insurance faces identity risk at every stage of the policy lifecycle

From ghost policies at inception to fraudulent claims at settlement, identity verification failures cost the Swiss insurance sector hundreds of millions annually.

Ghost policy and identity fraud at inception

Fraudsters submit insurance applications using stolen or fabricated identities — ghost policies that pay out fraudulently without a genuine insured party. Traditional paper-based application processes have minimal identity verification, creating an exploitable entry point.

Claims fraud from misrepresentation and substitution

Claims submitted by individuals other than the genuine policyholder, or supported by falsified identity documentation, represent a significant proportion of insurance fraud. Biometric verification at claims submission confirms the claimant is the actual policyholder.

AML obligations for life insurance under GwG

Swiss life insurance products — particularly investment-linked and single-premium policies with cash surrender value — are GwG-regulated financial products. Insurers must identify policyholders and beneficial owners, screen against SECO, and maintain AML documentation.

Medical identity fraud in health products

In health and disability insurance, identity fraud manifests as benefit diversion — payments for services rendered to one person are claimed under another's policy. Biometric identity verification at claims point prevents this class of fraud.

Beneficiary and consent verification

Life insurance beneficiary designations and policy changes require verified consent from the policyholder. Without biometric verification, fraudulent beneficiary changes — typically by bad actors with access to the policyholder's personal information — go undetected until claims time.

Cross-border policyholder verification

Swiss insurers, particularly in the life and private banking-adjacent segment, serve international policyholders. Verifying identity documents from 50+ countries with different formats, languages, and authentication standards requires comprehensive global document coverage.

The VerfiX Approach

How VerfiX protects the full insurance policy lifecycle

From digital onboarding to claims verification, VerfiX provides identity assurance at every stage where fraud risk is highest.

Digital policyholder onboarding — fully compliant

Replace paper application forms with a digital identity verification flow. The policyholder photographs their identity document and takes a selfie. VerfiX verifies the document, confirms biometric match and liveness, screens against AML lists, and returns a structured compliance record. No in-person meeting required.

Claims fraud prevention — biometric match at submission

At claims submission, VerfiX re-verifies that the claimant is the genuine policyholder through biometric face matching. The claimant's current selfie is compared against the biometric record from onboarding. Any mismatch flags the claim for investigation before payment is authorised.

Beneficiary and policy change consent verification

Sensitive policy changes — beneficiary designation, loan against policy value, premium redirection — require biometric consent from the policyholder. VerfiX provides a consent verification flow: the policyholder confirms their identity biometrically, generating an auditable consent record that protects against fraudulent policy manipulation.

AML compliance for GwG-regulated life products

For life insurance products subject to GwG, VerfiX handles the full AML compliance requirement: policyholder identification, SECO and global sanctions screening, PEP identification with EDD workflow, ongoing monitoring, and immutable audit trail generation.

Third-party payer UBO identification

When premiums are paid by a third party — a common structure in executive and key-person insurance — GwG requires identification of the beneficial owner of the policy. VerfiX KYB and UBO identification handles third-party payer structures with the same automation as direct policyholder onboarding.

Fraud analytics from verification signals

Aggregated fraud signals from the verification layer — document patterns, device intelligence, geographic anomalies, velocity checks — enable insurers to identify fraud rings operating multiple applications. These signals are available through the VerfiX API alongside individual verification results.

Business Benefits

Measurable outcomes from day one

Claims fraud losses significantly reduced

Biometric re-verification at claims submission catches identity substitution fraud before payment is made. Post-payment fraud recovery costs 10-20x more than pre-payment prevention. The ROI of biometric claims verification is measurable within the first quarter of deployment.

Digital onboarding — no in-person appointment required

Policyholders complete identity verification from any device in under three minutes. Elimination of in-person onboarding requirements expands addressable market and reduces acquisition cost — particularly for affinity, embedded, and direct-to-consumer insurance distribution.

GwG audit trail without manual work

For life insurance products, the GwG compliance documentation for every policyholder is generated automatically. The AML audit trail required for FINMA inspection is always current and export-ready — no manual file assembly before regulatory reviews.

Policyholder consent on record for disputes

Biometric consent records for sensitive policy actions — beneficiary changes, policy loans, premium redirection — provide legally defensible evidence of policyholder authorisation. These records protect insurers in dispute resolution and regulatory investigations.

AML compliance without dedicated compliance headcount

The VerfiX Trust Engine automates AML screening, ongoing monitoring, and audit trail generation. For smaller insurers without dedicated AML teams, this provides institutional-grade compliance infrastructure at a fraction of the cost of building it internally.

nFADP-compliant biometric data handling

Insurance companies process biometric data for policyholder verification. VerfiX ensures nFADP compliance: biometric templates deleted post-session, legal basis documentation provided, Swiss DPA in place before any processing begins.

Verification Workflow

From request to verified result in seconds

VerfiX slots into your existing stack. No rearchitecting. One REST API call returns document data, biometric result, AML screening outcome, and a FINMA-compliant audit record.

01

Policyholder initiates application

Customer begins policy application online or through an insurance broker portal. The insurer's system calls the VerfiX API to initiate an identity verification session.

02

Identity verification — document and biometric

Policyholder photographs their identity document and takes a selfie with liveness check. VerfiX verifies the document, performs biometric matching, and screens against AML lists — all in under three seconds.

03

AML screening and risk scoring

Trust Engine screens the verified identity against SECO, UN, OFAC, and PEP databases. The Trust Score incorporates document validity, biometric confidence, AML result, and fraud signals. Automated decision applied.

04

Policy issued — audit trail created

Approved applicants proceed to policy issuance. The complete KYC and AML audit trail is stored in Swiss infrastructure. For GwG-regulated products, ongoing monitoring begins automatically.

05

Claims — biometric re-verification

At claims submission, the claimant is re-verified biometrically. The current selfie is compared to the onboarding record. Match confirmed → claim proceeds. Mismatch → flagged for investigation before payment.
