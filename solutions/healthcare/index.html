Industry Challenges

Healthcare identity verification is a patient safety issue

Medical identity fraud is not a financial crime — it is a patient safety risk. Treatment decisions based on another patient's medical record can be life-threatening.

Medical identity fraud in health systems

Medical identity fraud — using another person's health insurance credentials to access services — contaminates medical records with another patient's treatment history. Treatment decisions made on corrupted medical records pose direct patient safety risks.

Health record access control at digital scale

Patient portal access, electronic health record sharing, and telemedicine platforms require robust identity verification. Password-based authentication is insufficient for the sensitivity of health data — particularly given the value of health data in identity fraud markets.

Health insurance fraud — benefits diversion

Health benefits diversion — claiming services under another person's insurance — is one of the most common forms of healthcare fraud. Biometric verification at service access or claims submission confirms the person using the insurance is the genuine policyholder.

Prescription fraud and controlled substance access

Fraudulent access to prescriptions and pharmacy records through identity impersonation is a growing concern in digital pharmacy and e-prescription systems. Identity verification at prescription access prevents diversion.

Health data privacy — the highest protection category

Under nFADP and GDPR, health data is the most sensitive category of personal data. Any identity verification system used in healthcare must handle biometric data with the strictest privacy controls — legal basis documentation, data minimisation, and minimal retention.

Telemedicine patient identity assurance

Remote medical consultations require confidence that the patient is who they claim to be. A misidentified patient in a telemedicine consultation may receive inappropriate treatment, medications, or referrals.

The VerfiX Approach

How VerfiX secures patient identity across the healthcare journey

From initial patient registration to each digital health interaction, VerfiX provides identity assurance at the points where fraud and patient safety risk are highest.

Digital patient registration — accurate from the start

New patient registration with document verification and biometric matching creates an accurate patient identity record at the foundation of the medical record. Preventing fraudulent registration at the beginning avoids downstream record contamination and treatment errors.

Health insurance fraud prevention at service access

At each service access point — clinic check-in, pharmacy benefit use, telehealth session start — VerfiX biometric re-verification confirms the patient is the genuine insurance policyholder. A biometric mismatch flags the encounter for investigation before services are rendered.

Telemedicine patient identity assurance

Before a teleconsultation begins, the patient completes a rapid biometric verification. The clinician receives confirmation that the patient in the video call is the same person whose medical record is displayed. This prevents consultation and prescription fraud.

Health portal access — stronger than passwords

Patient portal login secured by biometric verification is significantly stronger than password authentication. Access to sensitive health records — diagnoses, prescriptions, test results — is protected against account takeover even if login credentials are compromised.

Health data privacy by design

VerfiX processes biometric data with the strictest privacy controls appropriate for a healthcare context: biometric templates deleted at session end, health data never transmitted to VerfiX infrastructure, legal basis documented, DPIA support provided, Swiss data residency confirmed.

E-prescription and pharmacy verification

For digital pharmacy and e-prescription platforms, VerfiX verifies that the patient collecting or accessing a prescription is the genuine prescription holder. Biometric identity check at prescription access or delivery prevents controlled substance diversion and prescription fraud.

Verification Workflow

From request to verified result in seconds

VerfiX slots into your existing stack. No rearchitecting. One REST API call returns document data, biometric result, AML screening outcome, and a FINMA-compliant audit record.

01

Patient initiates registration or service access

Patient accesses digital registration, portal login, or telemedicine platform. System calls VerfiX API to initiate patient identity verification at appropriate assurance level.

02

Identity document verification

Patient photographs health insurance card and identity document. VerfiX verifies document authenticity and extracts structured patient identity data — name, date of birth, AHV number where present.

03

Biometric confirmation

Patient takes a selfie with liveness check. VerfiX confirms biometric match. For returning patients, match is performed against the original registration biometric record.

04

Service access granted — encounter documented

Verified patient proceeds to service. Verification result — including match confidence and liveness result — is recorded in the encounter record as part of the clinical audit trail.

05

Biometric data deleted — identity record retained

Biometric processing data is deleted at session end per nFADP data minimisation requirements. The verification outcome record — non-biometric — is retained in the patient's audit trail for the required period.

Under nFADP, processing biometric data in a healthcare context may be justified under: (1) the health treatment purpose, where biometric verification is necessary for the provision of health services; (2) explicit consent from the patient; or (3) legal obligation, where applicable sectoral law requires identity verification.
