Industry Challenges

Government digital transformation requires trust infrastructure

Moving public services online without robust identity verification creates fraud risk, data breaches, and legal liability. The solution must be accessible, inclusive, and legally defensible.

High-stakes identity decisions at scale

Government identity decisions — benefits eligibility, professional licensing, permit issuance — have significant legal and financial consequences. Manual identity verification creates bottlenecks; insufficient digital verification creates fraud exposure.

eIDAS and eID legal framework compliance

Swiss public authorities must align with the Swiss eID Act and, for EU-connected services, with eIDAS 2.0 requirements. Accepting EU Digital Identity Wallet credentials — eIDAS High assurance — requires a technically compliant acceptance infrastructure.

Privacy law obligations — highest sensitivity data

Government processing of biometric data is subject to strict nFADP requirements and heightened public scrutiny. Privacy-by-design architecture, minimal data retention, and documented legal basis are non-negotiable.

Residents with foreign identity documents

Swiss authorities serve a population of which 25%+ are foreign nationals. Verifying identity documents from 190+ countries — including the Ausländerausweis, EU national ID cards, and non-European passports — requires comprehensive global document coverage.

Inclusive access for all residents

Digital government services must be accessible to elderly, disabled, and digitally less-connected residents. The identity verification flow must be simple enough for all residents to complete without in-person assistance.

Integration with legacy government IT systems

Government IT environments include legacy systems that cannot easily adopt modern authentication. The identity verification layer must integrate with existing eGovernment infrastructure rather than requiring system replacement.

The VerfiX Approach

How VerfiX enables trusted digital government services

From the simplest permit application to the highest-assurance identity decisions, VerfiX provides the appropriate verification level with full privacy compliance.

Highest assurance identity verification — NFC chip reading

For high-stakes government decisions — professional licensing, residence permit issuance, benefit access — VerfiX uses NFC chip reading to validate the digital signature on ePassports and modern identity cards. Government-signed chip data provides the highest available assurance level — stronger than any visual document check.

Swiss eID and EU Digital Identity Wallet acceptance

VerfiX eID Gateway accepts Swiss eID credentials and EU Digital Identity Wallet (EUDIW) presentations from all 27 EU member states. Citizens present their eID credential from their mobile wallet; VerfiX validates the cryptographic credential against the issuing country's trust anchor and returns verified identity attributes.

Privacy-first architecture for sensitive government data

Government biometric data processing requires the most privacy-protective architecture. VerfiX default configuration: biometric data deleted at session end, no persistent biometric storage, data minimisation applied to every field, legal basis documented before any processing begins. DPIA support documentation is included.

Resident population coverage — all nationalities

Swiss residents include citizens and long-term residents from 180+ countries. VerfiX verifies all Swiss identity documents plus international passports, EU national ID cards, and the Ausländerausweis (all permit types). Every resident can be verified digitally regardless of their document's country of origin.

Integration with existing eGovernment infrastructure

VerfiX integrates via REST API with any eGovernment platform, citizen portal, or administrative system. The platform supports SAML 2.0 and OpenID Connect for identity federation. Government IT teams receive a full integration package including security documentation for procurement approval.

Legally defensible administrative identity records

Every identity verification event generates an immutable, timestamped record with all evidence: document data, biometric match result, liveness confirmation, and verification metadata. This record satisfies administrative law requirements for documented identity decisions and is defensible in appeal and judicial review proceedings.

Business Benefits

Measurable outcomes from day one

Digital service adoption accelerated

Residents complete identity verification in under three minutes from any device. Removing the requirement for in-person identity appointments significantly increases digital service adoption rates, particularly among younger and internationally mobile residents.

Benefit fraud and identity theft prevented

Biometric verification at service access prevents fraudulent benefit claims using stolen identity data. The combination of document verification, face match, and liveness detection closes the primary attack vectors used in government identity fraud.

Administrative processing cost reduced

Manual identity verification — scheduling appointments, training staff, processing documents — is replaced by an automated digital flow. Government agencies redeploy staff from identity checking to higher-value citizen service activities.

Legal basis for administrative identity decisions

The VerfiX audit trail provides legally defensible records for administrative decisions based on identity verification. In appeal, judicial review, and data protection investigations, the structured verification record demonstrates the basis and quality of the identity decision.

Privacy compliance from day one

Government agencies face the highest scrutiny on privacy law compliance. VerfiX's privacy-by-design architecture, DPIA support documentation, and minimal data retention defaults make nFADP compliance demonstrably achievable from deployment.

Compatible with Swiss eGovernment infrastructure

VerfiX supports the standard identity federation protocols (OpenID Connect, SAML 2.0) used in Swiss eGovernment architecture. Integration with existing citizen portal and authentication infrastructure does not require system replacement.

Verification Workflow

From request to verified result in seconds

VerfiX slots into your existing stack. No rearchitecting. One REST API call returns document data, biometric result, AML screening outcome, and a FINMA-compliant audit record.

01

Citizen accesses digital government service

The citizen visits the government service portal — benefit application, permit renewal, professional licensing. The portal calls the VerfiX API to initiate an identity verification session at the required assurance level.

02

Identity document verification

The citizen photographs their identity document or presents their eID credential. VerfiX verifies document authenticity, reads the MRZ and optionally the NFC chip, and extracts verified identity attributes.

03

Biometric confirmation

The citizen takes a selfie with liveness check. VerfiX performs biometric matching against the document photograph. Active or passive liveness confirms a live person — preventing impersonation using photographs.

04

Assurance level confirmed — service access granted

The verification result is returned to the government portal with the achieved assurance level (Substantial or High), verified identity attributes, and the audit record identifier. The portal grants appropriate service access.

05

Audit record retained for administrative purposes

The complete verification record is retained in Swiss infrastructure for the period required by applicable record retention law. Exportable for audit, inspection, or judicial review on request.
