Industry Challenges

Crypto compliance is no longer optional — it is existential

Swiss VASPs face the same AML obligations as banks, with less compliance infrastructure, faster transaction velocity, and a customer base that spans the globe.

GwG AML obligations identical to banks

FINMA has clarified that Swiss VASPs — crypto exchanges, custody providers, DeFi interface operators — are financial intermediaries subject to the full GwG framework. KYC at onboarding, UBO identification, SECO screening, ongoing monitoring, and 10-year record retention are all required.

Global customer base, diverse documents

Crypto customers are inherently global. A Swiss exchange onboards customers from 50+ countries in the first month. Verifying identity documents from this diversity — passports, ID cards, driving licences across every jurisdiction — requires a comprehensive document library, not a handful of supported countries.

FATF grey list and sanctions velocity

The crypto sector has faced disproportionate sanctions risk from jurisdictional grey listing and individual designations. OFAC has designated individual crypto wallets and entities. Sanctions lists relevant to crypto move faster than lists in traditional finance.

Travel Rule technical complexity

The FATF Travel Rule (FINMA threshold: CHF 1,000) requires VASPs to collect and transmit originator and beneficiary information with crypto transfers. The verified identity records from KYC onboarding are the data source that Travel Rule systems require.

Anonymous and pseudonymous actors

Crypto-native users have a higher-than-average proportion of privacy-motivated actors, some of whom use techniques to obscure identity. Liveness detection, deepfake detection, and synthetic identity signals are particularly important in this context.

FINMA inspection readiness for VASPs

Following Switzerland's 2024 FATF grey listing, FINMA has explicitly targeted VASP compliance programmes. Exchanges without structured KYC documentation, ongoing monitoring evidence, and systematic AML records face enforcement risk at the next supervisory review.

The VerfiX Approach

How VerfiX enables compliant crypto onboarding at speed

Crypto customers expect sub-minute onboarding. Compliance teams expect complete documentation. VerfiX delivers both simultaneously.

Global KYC in under three minutes

The customer photographs their identity document and takes a selfie. VerfiX verifies the document against a library of 4,500+ document types from 190+ countries, performs biometric matching and liveness detection, and returns a structured result in under three seconds. The customer sees a decision in under three minutes.

SECO and global sanctions in real time

Every verification triggers simultaneous screening against SECO, UN Security Council, OFAC SDN, EU Consolidated List, HM Treasury, and PEP databases. Crypto-specific sanctions designations — individual wallet operators, exchange entities — are included in the monitored lists.

Automated ongoing monitoring for the full customer base

The entire customer base is rescreened automatically at configurable intervals — daily for high-risk, weekly for standard. New designations relevant to crypto are reflected in the next scheduled rescan. New matches trigger webhook alerts with structured review workflow.

Travel Rule originator data — ready to hand off

The verified identity record generated by VerfiX — confirmed name, date of birth, nationality, document number — is structured exactly as required by Travel Rule messaging protocols. Integrating a Travel Rule solution alongside VerfiX requires only a data mapping layer, not a new identity verification workflow.

Deepfake and synthetic identity detection

Crypto onboarding attracts a higher rate of fraudulent identity submissions than traditional finance. VerfiX Fraud Intelligence includes dedicated deepfake detection, synthetic identity signals, and document fraud analysis — critical for an inherently digital, anonymous-preference customer segment.

FINMA-inspection-ready VASP documentation

Every verification, every AML screening event, every ongoing monitoring rescan, and every alert review is logged in an immutable audit trail. The complete compliance programme documentation for any customer or time period is exportable in structured format for FINMA inspection.

Verification Workflow

From request to verified result in seconds

VerfiX slots into your existing stack. No rearchitecting. One REST API call returns document data, biometric result, AML screening outcome, and a FINMA-compliant audit record.

01

Customer initiates account registration

The customer signs up on the exchange's web or mobile interface. The exchange calls the VerfiX API to create a KYC session. A verification link or in-app flow is presented to the customer.

02

Document and biometric capture

The customer photographs their identity document and takes a selfie with liveness check. VerfiX processes both in real time — document classification, OCR, MRZ, security features, face match, liveness — in under three seconds.

03

Real-time SECO and global sanctions screening

The verified identity is immediately screened against all configured lists — SECO, UN, OFAC, EU, PEP. Any hits are flagged with match details and confidence scores. Clear results are returned with the verification response.

04

Trust Score and automated onboarding decision

The Trust Score is calculated and the decision applied. Low-risk customers are approved immediately. Review cases are queued. Sanctions matches are auto-rejected with audit record creation.

05

Exchange account activated and ongoing monitoring starts

Approved customers have their exchange account activated. VerfiX begins ongoing monitoring — scheduling automatic rescreenings at the interval configured for the customer's risk tier. New matches trigger webhook alerts to the compliance team.
