Industry Challenges

The compliance burden Swiss banks carry every day

Regulatory pressure, rising fraud, and digital customer expectations create a difficult triangle for Swiss banking compliance teams.

FINMA Circular 2016/7 technical obligations

Online identification requires document verification with security feature checks, biometric face matching, liveness detection, and an immutable audit trail. Meeting all four simultaneously with manual processes is operationally unsustainable.

GwG beneficial owner identification

GwG Article 4 requires identification of UBOs for all legal entity relationships. Manually tracing ownership chains across jurisdictions delays onboarding and exposes gaps that FINMA inspectors target first.

AML grey-list scrutiny

Following Switzerland's 2024 FATF grey listing, FINMA has increased supervisory intensity. AML programme documentation quality — ongoing monitoring evidence, UBO files, alert management records — is under greater scrutiny than ever.

KYC refresh at scale

Maintaining current KYC files across tens of thousands of customers — with risk-tiered refresh cycles — is one of the largest operational challenges in Swiss retail banking compliance.

Identity fraud and synthetic identities

Digital-first onboarding creates surface area for document fraud, deepfakes, and synthetic identity attacks. Traditional checks built for in-branch processes do not transfer directly to digital channels.

Cross-border client complexity

Swiss private and cantonal banks serve international clients whose identity documents, beneficial ownership structures, and AML risk profiles span dozens of jurisdictions — each requiring appropriate verification methodology.

The VerfiX Approach

How VerfiX solves the Swiss banking compliance challenge

VerfiX replaces disconnected point solutions with a unified compliance API built around the Swiss regulatory framework. One integration covers the full KYC, KYB, AML, and audit trail obligation.

FINMA Circular 2016/7-compliant Online Identification

The VerfiX KYC flow meets all four Online Identification requirements: document verification with security feature checks, MRZ validation, biometric face match at ISO 30107-3 standard, and active+passive liveness detection. The audit trail is generated automatically.

Automated KYB with Swiss company registry

VerfiX integrates with Zefix for live Swiss company registry lookups. Ownership chains are mapped automatically to UBO level. All identified UBOs are routed through the KYC flow. The complete KYB file with registry extract is retained in the audit trail.

Real-time and ongoing AML screening

Every customer is screened against SECO, UN Security Council, OFAC, HM Treasury, EU Consolidated List, and multiple PEP databases at onboarding. The entire book of business is automatically rescreened at configurable intervals. New matches trigger webhook alerts with structured review workflow.

Trust Engine risk scoring for automated decisions

The Trust Score aggregates all verification signals — document validity, biometric confidence, liveness, AML, fraud indicators, jurisdiction risk — into a single 0-100 score. Banks configure approval, review, and rejection thresholds. Hard-stop rules handle sanctions matches regardless of score.

Digital KYC refresh without branch visits

For periodic KYC refresh, banks send customers a verification link. The customer completes document and biometric re-verification in under three minutes from any device. The updated verification record and audit trail are immediately available. No branch visit, no manual paperwork.

FINMA inspection-ready in under one hour

The complete KYC audit trail for any customer — identity verification evidence, AML screening history, ongoing monitoring logs — can be exported in structured format within one hour of any regulatory request. This replaces days of manual file assembly before inspections.

Verification Workflow

From request to verified result in seconds

VerfiX slots into your existing stack. No rearchitecting. One REST API call returns document data, biometric result, AML screening outcome, and a FINMA-compliant audit record.

01

Customer initiates account application

The customer begins an account opening application on the bank's web or mobile interface. The bank's system calls the VerfiX API to initiate a KYC session and returns a verification URL or SDK token to the customer.

02

Document capture and verification

The customer photographs their identity document — front and back. VerfiX classifies the document, reads and validates the MRZ, checks security features, and extracts structured identity data. Processing time: under two seconds.

03

Biometric face match and liveness detection

The customer takes a selfie. VerfiX performs a biometric comparison between the live selfie and the document photograph. Active and passive liveness detection confirms the capture is from a real, live person. Processing: under one second.

04

AML screening

VerfiX Trust Engine screens the verified identity against SECO, UN, OFAC, EU, HM Treasury, and PEP databases simultaneously. The bank receives a structured screening result with match confidence scores and any alert details.

05

Trust Score and automated decision

The platform generates a composite Trust Score from all signals. Customers above the approval threshold are auto-approved. Those in the review band are queued for compliance analyst review. Sanctions matches are auto-rejected regardless of score.

06

Structured result and audit trail delivered

The bank's system receives a structured JSON response with all verification data, scores, decisions, and the audit record identifier. The complete audit trail — FINMA-ready — is stored in Swiss infrastructure for the GwG 10-year retention period.
