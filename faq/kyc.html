KYC FAQ

Know Your Customer obligations, processes, and technology for Swiss regulated institutions.

KYC (Know Your Customer) requires financial intermediaries to identify customers, verify identity, and document the process under GwG Art. 3-8. FINMA Circular 2016/7 governs remote digital identification. Obligations include identification, documentation, and ten-year retention of all KYC records.

KYC — Know Your Customer — is the legal and procedural framework by which regulated organisations verify customer identity and assess their risk profile. In Switzerland, KYC obligations arise primarily from the Federal Act on Combating Money Laundering (GwG/AMLA).

Key GwG provisions: Art. 3 (identification of contracting party before relationship), Art. 4 (identification of beneficial owner), Art. 6 (clarification obligation for unclear transactions), Art. 7 (documentation), Art. 8 (10-year retention), Art. 13 (enhanced due diligence for high-risk).

FINMA Circular 2016/7 defines how identification may be performed remotely — through document verification, biometric matching, and liveness detection.

VerfiX is designed to fulfil all GwG KYC obligations: document verification for Art. 3, KYB/UBO flow for Art. 4, immutable audit trail for Art. 7, configurable retention for Art. 8, and EDD workflow for Art. 13.

Refresh frequency is risk-based: high-risk customers (PEPs, high-risk jurisdictions) annually; medium-risk every two to three years; standard-risk every three to five years. Event-triggered refresh is required on material changes regardless of schedule. VerfiX enables fully digital refresh without branch visits.

KYC refresh — periodic re-verification of existing customers — is required to maintain the currency of identity records and adapt to changes in customer risk profile. The GwG requires maintenance of current information and refresh when circumstances change materially.

Risk-based refresh schedule: High-risk (PEPs, high-risk jurisdictions, complex structures): annual refresh. Medium-risk: 2-3 year cycle. Standard-risk: 3-5 year cycle.

Event-triggered refresh: required regardless of schedule when a beneficial owner changes; a customer becomes a PEP; a transaction profile changes materially; or a new AML hit is generated.

VerfiX supports fully digital KYC refresh. The organisation sends a verification link; the customer completes document and biometric verification from their device in under three minutes; the updated record is immediately available. No branch visit required.

Digital refresh reduces per-customer operational cost significantly and achieves higher completion rates because it can be done at any time and location.

EDD is elevated KYC scrutiny for high-risk customers — PEPs, high-risk jurisdictions, complex ownership structures. It requires additional source of funds verification, senior management approval, and enhanced ongoing monitoring. VerfiX Trust Engine triggers configurable EDD workflows automatically when risk thresholds are exceeded.

Enhanced Due Diligence is required by GwG Art. 13 for business relationships that present elevated money laundering or terrorist financing risk. It goes beyond standard KYC to gather deeper information about the customer's source of funds, source of wealth, and purpose of the relationship.

EDD triggers: PEP status (or family members/close associates); customer from or transacting with FATF-listed high-risk jurisdiction; complex/opaque ownership structure; correspondent banking relationships; any relationship where suspicion exists.

EDD components: source of funds and wealth documentation; enhanced ongoing monitoring; senior management approval; more frequent periodic review.

VerfiX Trust Engine supports EDD through configurable risk-score thresholds and hard-stop triggers. When a PEP match, high-risk jurisdiction flag, or sub-threshold Trust Score is detected, the case is automatically routed to a compliance analyst with a structured EDD workflow. All EDD steps and findings are documented in the audit trail for FINMA inspection readiness.

Identity verification is a technical process: confirming a document is genuine and the person matches it. KYC is a broader regulatory obligation that includes identity verification plus beneficial owner identification, risk assessment, AML screening, ongoing monitoring, documentation, and retention. VerfiX covers the full KYC stack.

Identity verification is a component of KYC, not a synonym for it. Identity verification addresses the technical question: is this document genuine, and does this person match it? KYC is the complete regulatory programme that uses identity verification as its foundation.

KYC components: (1) Identity verification: document + biometrics + liveness. (2) Beneficial owner identification: for entities, tracing ownership to natural persons. (3) Risk assessment: scoring the customer's AML risk profile. (4) AML screening: checking against sanctions, PEP, and adverse media lists. (5) Ongoing monitoring: continuous rescreening and transaction monitoring. (6) Documentation: maintaining the complete KYC file. (7) Retention: storing records for the legally required period.

VerfiX covers the full KYC stack through its integrated products: KYC module (identity verification), KYB module (beneficial owner identification), Trust Engine (risk scoring, AML screening, ongoing monitoring), and the immutable audit trail (documentation and retention).
