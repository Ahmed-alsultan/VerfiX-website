Identity Verification FAQ

Core concepts and requirements for digital identity verification under Swiss and European regulation.

Identity verification confirms a person is who they claim to be. Swiss law — specifically GwG Art. 3 and FINMA Circular 2016/7 — requires financial intermediaries to verify customer identity before entering into a business relationship. Failure to comply exposes institutions to regulatory enforcement and reputational damage.

Identity verification is the process of confirming that an individual genuinely is who they present themselves to be, using authoritative identity evidence such as a government-issued document and a biometric comparison. For regulated organisations, it is a legal obligation under the Federal Act on Combating Money Laundering (GwG) and a technical standard under FINMA Circular 2016/7.

Modern identity verification automates what was previously a manual, in-branch process. A customer photographs their identity document, takes a selfie, and the platform validates document authenticity, extracts data, performs a biometric face match, and confirms liveness — all in under three seconds. The result is a FINMA-compliant audit trail generated automatically for every verification event.

Beyond compliance, identity verification reduces onboarding fraud, accelerates customer acquisition, and creates a trusted foundation for every subsequent customer interaction. Institutions implementing automated identity verification typically reduce manual review time by over 80% while achieving higher accuracy than manual processes.

FINMA Circular 2016/7 defines the technical requirements for remote video and online identification in Switzerland. It mandates document verification with security feature checks, biometric face matching, and liveness detection. VerfiX implements all requirements and generates a FINMA-compliant audit trail automatically.

FINMA Circular 2016/7 — 'Video and Online Identification' — establishes the technical and procedural standards that Swiss financial institutions must meet when identifying customers through digital channels rather than in person. It defines two methods: Video Identification (human agent via video call) and Online Identification (fully automated).

For Online Identification, the requirements are: (1) document verification including security feature checks and MRZ validation; (2) biometric face matching between a live selfie and the document photograph; (3) liveness detection to prevent presentation attacks; and (4) an immutable audit trail with all verification evidence retained.

VerfiX implements all four requirements natively. The platform's document verification covers 4,500+ document types, biometric matching uses ISO 30107-3 compliant algorithms, liveness detection covers both active and passive methods, and the audit trail is generated automatically — exportable for FINMA inspection on demand.

VerfiX is engineered for production-grade performance. The API round-trip for a complete KYC verification is typically under three seconds from submission to structured result. VerfiX supports 4,500+ document types from 190+ countries: passports, national ID cards, driving licences, residence permits, and visas. All Swiss document types — passport, identity card, driving licence, Ausländerausweis B/C/L — are natively supported with full MRZ and security feature validation.

VerfiX maintains a comprehensive document template library covering over 4,500 distinct document types from more than 190 countries. The library is continuously updated as countries introduce new document versions, security features, and digital elements.

Swiss documents fully supported include: Swiss passport (all versions), Swiss identity card (Identitätskarte), Swiss driving licence, Ausländerausweis B, C, F, G, L, and N.

International categories include: ICAO 9303-compliant passports (including NFC ePassports), EU and EEA national identity cards, EU driving licences, UK driving licence, major non-EU driving licences (US all 50 states, Australian, Canadian, UAE), UN refugee travel documents, Schengen and national visas, UK Biometric Residence Permits.

The document classification engine automatically identifies the document type before applying the appropriate verification template, handling significant layout and security feature variation across jurisdictions.
