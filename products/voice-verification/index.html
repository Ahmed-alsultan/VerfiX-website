Voice Verification

VerfiX Voice Verification analyses the unique biometric characteristics of the human voice. The system creates an encrypted Voice Print during initial enrolment and compares the caller's voice in real time on every authentication. Anti-spoofing, deepfake detection and configurable decision thresholds make VerfiX the most secure voice verification solution for Swiss financial institutions.

Everything about VerfiX Voice Verification — technology, regulation and integration.

VerfiX Voice Verification analyses the unique biometric characteristics of the human voice — pitch, speech rhythm, formants, and voice quality. During initial enrolment, the system creates an encrypted voice model (Voice Print). On every subsequent authentication, the caller's voice is compared in real time against this model and a confidence score is computed.

The system reliably detects spoofing attempts, replay attacks, and synthetic voice generation by AI models (Deepfake Audio).

Yes. VerfiX Voice Verification is designed as an additional authentication factor in accordance with FINMA Circular 2016/7. It complements existing identification processes and is particularly suited to telephone banking and call centre authentication under Swiss AML (GwG) requirements.

The system records every verification event immutably — audit logs are FINMA audit-ready and exportable on demand.

The active verification phase typically takes 3–8 seconds. The full process including database lookup and score calculation completes in under 2 seconds — fully transparent to the caller.

Initial enrolment requires approximately 15–30 seconds of speaking time and can be seamlessly integrated into existing onboarding flows.

All voice biometric data — in particular Voice Prints — is stored and processed exclusively in Switzerland. VerfiX supports Swiss data residency on dedicated servers or on-premise within your own infrastructure.

Voice Prints are never stored in plain text. They are held in an encrypted, non-reversible mathematical model (one-way feature vector) — the original voice cannot be reconstructed from it.

100% Swiss data storage

On failure (score below the configured threshold), several escalation options are available:

  • Second attempt: The caller is asked to repeat the verification phrase
  • Fallback authentication: Redirect to PIN/TAN or knowledge-based questions
  • Manual review: Escalation to a human agent with full context
  • Alert workflow: Automatic flagging and fraud team notification on suspected fraud

All decision thresholds are individually configurable — your compliance team sets the risk parameters.

VerfiX integrates active anti-spoofing detection at multiple levels:

  • Detection of replay attacks (recorded voices)
  • Detection of text-to-speech synthesis (TTS)
  • Detection of voice conversion (voice-changing software)
  • Detection of AI-generated deepfake audio (GAN-based systems)

Anti-spoofing models are continuously trained and updated with current attack vectors.
