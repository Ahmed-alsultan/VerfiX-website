Identity Verification for Telecom Operators

VerfiX for Telecom Operators

VerfiX provides telecom operators with API-based identity verification for SIM registration, subscriber onboarding, SIM-swap prevention, and voice biometric authentication — with Swiss data residency and regulatory compliance built in.

Telecom Identity Verification Requirements

Mobile network operators in Switzerland are subject to identity verification requirements for SIM registration and subscriber onboarding. As regulatory frameworks evolve toward stronger identity assurance requirements for telecommunications services, telecom operators need a scalable, automated solution that can:

  • Verify subscriber identity at SIM registration without requiring physical store visits
  • Prevent SIM-swap fraud — where an attacker obtains a new SIM card in the victim's name — which is a primary attack vector for account takeover and banking fraud
  • Authenticate existing subscribers for high-risk actions (plan changes, SIM replacement, account updates) using biometric factors stronger than PINs or passwords
  • Process identity verification at the scale of consumer mobile subscriptions without creating onboarding friction that increases subscriber churn

VerfiX Capabilities for Telecom

Capability Application in Telecom
Document Verification Verify subscriber identity at online SIM registration. Accepts all Swiss-issued ID documents plus 4,500+ international document types for roaming subscribers and international customers.
Face Verification & Liveness Biometric match between subscriber selfie and ID photograph. Liveness detection prevents photo or video replay attacks. Suitable for online SIM registration and high-risk account changes.

SIM-Swap Fraud Prevention

Diagram: SIM-Swap Prevention Flow — Subscriber requests SIM replacement → Telecom system calls VerfiX KYC API → Subscriber receives verification link → Completes document verification + biometric → VerfiX returns identity match result → If match: SIM replacement proceeds → If no match or low confidence: blocked or routed to human review → Audit trail generated

SIM-swap fraud is a significant and growing attack vector. Once an attacker obtains a SIM card in the victim's name, they can receive the victim's SMS-based two-factor authentication codes, enabling account takeover on banking, email, and social media accounts. VerfiX biometric verification creates an additional identity check that cannot be defeated with social engineering alone.

Voice Biometrics for Call Centres

Most telecom operator call centres authenticate subscribers using knowledge-based factors (PIN, mother's maiden name, last bill amount) that are vulnerable to social engineering. Voice biometrics provides a stronger authentication factor:

  • Enrolment: Subscriber's voiceprint is captured and stored during account registration or a dedicated enrolment call
  • Authentication: On subsequent service calls, the subscriber's voice is matched against the stored voiceprint in real time
  • Swiss German support: VerfiX Voice Biometrics supports Swiss German dialects, enabling deployment across all four Swiss language regions
  • Passive mode: Authentication occurs during natural conversation — no challenge phrase required — minimising disruption to call flow

Integration

VerfiX integrates with telecom OSS/BSS systems via REST API. Verification events can be triggered from:

  • Online self-service portal (digital SIM registration, account management)
  • Retail point-of-sale systems (in-store SIM registration with digital identity verification)
  • Call centre applications (voice biometric authentication during service calls)
  • Mobile apps (subscriber app biometric re-authentication for high-risk actions)
