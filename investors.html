The problem

Europe's identity and payment rails are fragmented and dependent.

Fragmented

Banks integrate many verification vendors per market, each with its own API, contract and data model.

Non-European

Core payment and verification infrastructure is largely controlled outside Europe, raising sovereignty concerns.

Privacy-exposed

Most biometric checks send sensitive data to external clouds — a growing tension with nFADP and GDPR.

Costly

Interchange and per-check fees stack up, and every new market means another integration.

The solution

One Swiss-made platform — on-premise and on-chip.

On-premiseVerification runs inside the bank's own environment — data stays in Switzerland.
On-chipFace and voice templates are processed on a secure element; raw biometrics never leave the device.
API-firstKYC, KYB, AML, Gateway and SDK behind one orchestration layer and one integration.
Why now

Regulation is forcing a once-in-a-decade reset.

eIDAS 2.0

The EU framework mandates member states to offer a European Digital Identity Wallet — adoption is rolling out toward 2026.

Swiss nFADP

Switzerland's revised data-protection law (in force since Sept 2023) raises the bar for how biometric data is handled.

Swiss e-ID Act

Switzerland's federal e-ID law was approved in 2024, with a state-issued e-ID planned — creating demand for compliant verification.

Sources: EU Regulation 2024/1183 (eIDAS 2.0); Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection (nFADP, in force 1 Sept 2023); Swiss Federal Act on Electronic Identity (e-ID, adopted 2024). Timelines per published government materials and may evolve.
