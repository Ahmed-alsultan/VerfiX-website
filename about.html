About VerfiX

Executive Summary

VerfiX AG is a Swiss identity infrastructure company providing a modular, API-first platform that enables regulated organisations to verify individual and business identities, screen against AML and sanctions lists, and make risk-based onboarding decisions — with all processing conducted exclusively in Switzerland.

The Opportunity

Regulated organisations across Switzerland face three converging pressures in 2025:

  • Regulatory intensity: Following Switzerland's FATF grey list designation, FINMA has significantly increased supervisory activity. KYC and AML programme quality is under greater scrutiny than at any previous point.
  • Digital transformation: Customers expect fully digital onboarding. Manual, paper-based identification is a competitive disadvantage and an operational cost that technology can eliminate.
  • Data sovereignty: Swiss financial institutions are under increasing pressure to ensure that personal and biometric data processed for compliance purposes does not leave Switzerland. Most global identity verification providers cannot satisfy this requirement.

VerfiX was built to address all three pressures through a single, Swiss-operated platform.

What VerfiX Does

The VerfiX platform performs four core functions:

Function Description Regulatory relevance
Identity Verification (KYC) Verifies the identity of individuals using document analysis, biometric face matching, and liveness detection GwG Art. 3, FINMA Circular 2016/7
Business Verification (KYB) Verifies legal entities, maps ownership structures, and identifies ultimate beneficial owners GwG Art. 4
eID Gateway Accepts EU Digital Identity Wallet credentials from all EU member states via a single API eIDAS 2.0 (designed for)
Trust Engine Scores risk, screens against AML/PEP/sanctions lists, and automates compliance decisions FATF, GwG, FINMA AML guidance

Target Market

VerfiX serves regulated organisations in Switzerland and Europe that are required by law or operational necessity to verify the identity of individuals or businesses. Primary sectors include banking, fintech, crypto platforms, insurance, telecom, government, universities, and mobility providers.

The immediate addressable market is Swiss financial intermediaries subject to GwG supervision — estimated at several thousand entities across banking, securities, insurance, and payment services.

Platform Architecture

VerfiX is API-first. Every capability is available through a documented REST API, enabling integration with existing core banking systems, CRM platforms, and onboarding workflows without proprietary middleware. The platform supports three deployment models:

  • Swiss-hosted cloud: Data processed and stored in certified Swiss data centres. Recommended for most organisations. Integration in under 48 hours.
  • Private cloud: Dedicated infrastructure for the customer within Swiss hosting. Higher isolation for sensitive deployments.
  • On-premise: Full deployment within the customer's own infrastructure for organisations with the strictest data control requirements.

Regulatory and Compliance Position

VerfiX is designed from the ground up for the Swiss regulatory environment:

  • FINMA Circular 2016/7: Platform is designed to meet remote identification requirements including document verification, biometric matching, and liveness detection.
  • GwG Art. 3 & 4: Designed to support customer and beneficial owner identification obligations.
  • nFADP: All processing in Switzerland; biometric data handled as sensitive personal data; Data Processing Agreement available.
  • GDPR: Architecture designed to support GDPR obligations for organisations with EU data subjects.
  • eIDAS 2.0: eID Gateway designed to accept European Digital Identity Wallet credentials as they roll out across EU member states.

Company Overview

Executive Summary

VerfiX AG is a Swiss identity infrastructure company that provides identity verification, business verification, risk scoring, and AML compliance capabilities to regulated organisations in Switzerland and Europe.

The company operates the VerfiX platform — a modular, API-first identity infrastructure designed to meet the compliance requirements of Swiss and European regulated industries. The platform processes identity verification decisions, business verification checks, and risk scoring using a combination of document analysis, biometric verification, and automated AML screening, with all data processing conducted exclusively in Switzerland.

VerfiX is positioned as Swiss-first identity infrastructure, addressing the specific requirements of organisations subject to FINMA supervision, the Swiss Federal Act on Combating Money Laundering (GwG), and the revised Federal Act on Data Protection (nFADP), while also supporting organisations with GDPR obligations.

Mission and Vision

Mission

Build the trust layer for digital identity verification in Switzerland and Europe.

Vision

To become the standard identity infrastructure for regulated industries across Switzerland and Europe — enabling every regulated organisation to verify who they are dealing with, confidently and in compliance, through a single Swiss-hosted platform.

Positioning

VerfiX occupies a differentiated position in the identity verification market: Swiss-first. While international identity verification providers offer global coverage from non-Swiss infrastructure, VerfiX is designed, built, and operated specifically for organisations that must comply with Swiss law, FINMA supervision, and Swiss data residency requirements. This makes VerfiX the natural choice for Swiss cantonal banks, Swiss fintechs, Swiss crypto platforms, and any regulated entity for which data sovereignty is a procurement requirement, not an option.

The Problem VerfiX Solves

Regulated organisations in Switzerland face a compliance environment that is more demanding than most, and growing more so. Following Switzerland's designation on the FATF grey list in 2024, FINMA has intensified supervision of KYC and AML programmes across all regulated sectors. At the same time, the revised nFADP introduced new obligations for organisations processing biometric data.

Existing identity verification solutions present three categories of difficulty for Swiss regulated entities:

  • Data sovereignty: Most major identity verification providers process and store data outside Switzerland, creating compliance risk under nFADP and FINMA supervisory expectations. Transferring biometric data to non-Swiss infrastructure requires explicit legal basis and enhanced documentation.
  • Swiss document and compliance coverage: International providers are optimised for the largest global markets. Swiss-specific document types, cantonal nuances, Swiss-German language support for voice biometrics, and alignment with GwG, FINMA, and nFADP require specific configuration that generic providers do not offer as standard.
  • Integration with Swiss systems: Swiss organisations use core banking systems, ERP platforms, and compliance tools that require integration patterns and data formats specific to the Swiss market. Generic API documentation does not address these requirements.

VerfiX addresses all three problems through a platform designed specifically for the Swiss regulatory environment, operated exclusively on Swiss infrastructure, with Swiss compliance built in by design rather than added as a configuration option.

Technical Overview

Architecture Approach

VerfiX is built as an API-first platform. All capabilities are exposed through a REST API, enabling integration with existing systems, core banking platforms, CRM tools, and custom onboarding workflows without requiring proprietary connectors. The platform is designed to function as infrastructure — not a standalone application — so that it augments and automates existing processes rather than replacing them.

Diagram: Architecture overview — Web/Mobile clients → REST API layer → KYC/KYB/Trust Engine modules → Swiss-hosted data layer → Audit trail storage

Deployment Options

  • Cloud (Swiss-hosted): Processing and storage in certified Swiss data centres. Fastest path to go-live. Recommended for most organisations.
  • Private Cloud: Dedicated infrastructure for the customer within a Swiss-hosted environment. Higher isolation for sensitive use cases.
  • On-Premise: Full deployment within the customer's own infrastructure. Designed for organisations with the strictest data control requirements.

Integration

  • REST API with JSON request and response format
  • API key and Bearer token authentication
  • Webhooks for asynchronous event notification (verification completed, AML hit, risk score update)
  • SDKs planned for Python, JavaScript, and Java
  • Sandbox environment for integration testing prior to production go-live
