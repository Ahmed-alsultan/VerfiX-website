eID Gateway Product Sheet

Single API for European Digital Identity Wallets

VerfiX eID Gateway

VerfiX eID Gateway is a single REST API integration that enables Swiss organisations to accept digital identity credentials from EU Digital Identity Wallets across all 27 EU member states, without building individual integrations for each country. Designed for the eIDAS 2.0 rollout.

The eIDAS 2.0 Context

eIDAS 2.0 (Regulation (EU) 2024/1183) mandates that all EU member states offer citizens a European Digital Identity Wallet (EUDIW) by 2026. The EUDIW allows EU citizens to store and present verified digital credentials — identity documents, driving licences, professional qualifications — from their mobile device, with cryptographic proof of authenticity issued by government authorities.

For Swiss organisations, eIDAS 2.0 creates both an opportunity and a technical challenge:

  • ›Opportunity: EU Digital Identity Wallet credentials are issued at the highest identity assurance level. Accepting them provides stronger identity assurance than traditional document scanning, potentially reducing fraud and streamlining onboarding.
  • ›Challenge: Each EU member state operates its own national wallet implementation. Accepting credentials from all 27 EU countries requires 27 separate technical integrations — unless a gateway handles the interoperability layer.

VerfiX eID Gateway is that gateway. One API call. All 27 EU wallets. Standardised result.

How VerfiX eID Gateway Works

⬡ Diagram: eID Gateway Flow — EU Citizen presents EUDIW credential on mobile → VerfiX eID Gateway receives credential presentation → Validates cryptographic signature against EU trust anchor → Verifies credential is current and unrevoked → Extracts identity attributes → Returns standardised identity result to Swiss organisation's system → Audit trail generated

The gateway abstracts the complexity of:

  • ›Validating the cryptographic signatures on EU wallet credentials against the correct national trust anchor for each of the 27 EU member states
  • ›Handling the different credential formats and attribute schemas used by different national implementations
  • ›Maintaining current trust lists as EU member states update their wallet infrastructure
  • ›Translating credential attributes into a standardised format compatible with Swiss onboarding workflows

Key Capabilities

Capability Description
27-country EU wallet coverage Designed to support EU Digital Identity Wallet credentials from all EU member states as they become available through the EUDIW rollout.
Cryptographic verification Validates digital signatures from government-issued EU wallet credentials against official EU trust anchors. Verifies credentials are current, unrevoked, and unmodified.
Attribute extraction Extracts identity attributes (name, date of birth, nationality, document number, address) from EU wallet credentials in a standardised format.
Highest assurance level EU Digital Identity Wallet credentials are issued at eIDAS High assurance level — the strongest identity assurance available under the EU framework.
Standardised output Returns a consistent, structured JSON response regardless of the source EU member state or credential format.
Swiss integration Output is designed to integrate directly with Swiss onboarding workflows and compliance systems. Audit trail generated for every credential verification.
FINMA compatibility Designed for compatibility with FINMA's evolving guidance on the acceptance of digital identity credentials for remote identification.

Use Cases

  • ›Swiss banks with EU clients: Accept EUDIW credentials for digital account opening from EU citizens, removing friction in cross-border onboarding.
  • ›Swiss fintechs with EU market ambitions: Integrate once and offer EU wallet-based onboarding to EU customers from day one.
