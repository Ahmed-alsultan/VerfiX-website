VerfiX AG — Information Security Framework

Information Security Policy — VerfiX AG

This Information Security Policy establishes the principles, objectives, and responsibilities for information security at VerfiX AG. It applies to all VerfiX personnel, contractors, and third-party providers with access to VerfiX systems, infrastructure, or customer data.

1. Policy Statement

VerfiX AG is committed to protecting the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of all information assets, including customer personal data processed through the VerfiX platform. As a company whose core business is the secure processing of sensitive identity and biometric data, VerfiX holds information security as a foundational business obligation, not an optional add-on.

This commitment is expressed through:

  • ›Maintaining ISO 27001-aligned information security management practices
  • ›Investing in security controls proportionate to the sensitivity of the data processed
  • ›Building security into platform design from the outset (security by design)
  • ›Maintaining transparency with customers about security controls and incidents

2. Security Objectives

Objective Description
Confidentiality Personal data and customer information is accessible only to those with an authorised need. Biometric data is not disclosed to any party other than the Controller on whose behalf it is processed.
Integrity Personal data and audit records are accurate and complete. Audit trail records are cryptographically protected against modification.
Availability The VerfiX platform is available to customers in accordance with the agreed SLA. Single points of failure are eliminated through redundant architecture.
Accountability All actions on the VerfiX platform are logged in an audit trail that enables identification of who did what, when, and to what data.

3. Asset Management

  • ›All information assets (hardware, software, data, and credentials) must be inventoried and assigned an owner
  • ›Information assets must be classified by sensitivity: Public, Internal, Confidential, or Restricted
  • ›Customer personal data is classified as Restricted and is subject to the highest level of access control
  • ›Information asset inventories must be reviewed annually

4. Access Control

  • ›Access to production systems and customer data is granted on a least-privilege basis
  • ›All production access requires multi-factor authentication
  • ›Access rights must be reviewed quarterly and revoked promptly on change of role or departure
  • ›Privileged access to production databases and infrastructure requires management approval and is logged in a separate privileged access audit trail
  • ›Remote access to VerfiX systems requires VPN and MFA
  • ›Shared accounts and service accounts are prohibited for human users

5. Cryptography

  • ›AES-256-GCM for all data at rest
  • ›TLS 1.3 for all data in transit (TLS 1.2 and below prohibited)
  • ›PBKDF2-SHA256 for credential storage
  • ›HMAC-SHA256 for webhook signatures
  • ›SHA-256 hash chain for audit trail integrity
  • ›Encryption keys managed via HSM with per-customer key isolation
  • ›Cryptographic standards reviewed and updated annually

6. Vulnerability Management

  • ›All software dependencies scanned for known vulnerabilities on each deployment
  • ›Static code analysis integrated into CI/CD pipeline
  • ›Annual penetration test by qualified third party (planned)
  • ›Critical vulnerabilities: patched within 24 hours
  • ›High vulnerabilities: patched within 7 days
  • ›Medium vulnerabilities: patched within 30 days
  • ›All identified vulnerabilities tracked in a security defect register

7. Personnel Security

  • ›Background verification appropriate to role conducted for all new personnel with access to customer data (where permitted by applicable law)
  • ›All personnel with access to customer data sign a confidentiality agreement before access is granted
  • ›Security awareness training mandatory for all personnel annually
  • ›Specific training on biometric data handling and nFADP obligations for personnel in processing roles
  • ›Personnel departure procedures include immediate revocation of all access rights and return of equipment

8. Physical Security

  • ›VerfiX relies on certified colocation data centre providers for physical infrastructure security
  • ›Data centre providers must maintain physical access controls, CCTV monitoring, and 24/7 security
  • ›No customer personal data is stored on portable or mobile devices
  • ›VerfiX office physical security measures are commensurate with the sensitivity of information handled

9. Supplier Security

  • ›All third-party providers with access to customer data must sign a data processing agreement
  • ›Sub-processors are assessed for security capability before engagement
  • ›Security requirements are included in all supplier contracts
  • ›Supplier security is reviewed annually or on material change

10. Policy Compliance and Review

Compliance with this policy is mandatory for all VerfiX personnel and contractors. Violations may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination. This policy will be reviewed at least annually and updated to reflect changes in the threat environment, technology, or applicable regulatory requirements.
