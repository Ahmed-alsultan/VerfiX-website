Trust Engine Product Sheet

Risk Scoring, AML Screening & Decision Intelligence

VerfiX Trust Engine

VerfiX Trust Engine is the risk intelligence and AML compliance layer of the VerfiX platform. It aggregates signals from identity verification, biometric matching, document analysis, and external AML databases into a single Trust Score and automated compliance decision.

Executive Summary

Effective AML compliance requires more than a sanctions list check at onboarding. It requires continuous monitoring, multi-source risk aggregation, configurable decision rules, and a complete audit trail that can satisfy a FINMA inspection on demand. Most organisations manage this with a combination of manual processes, disconnected tools, and spreadsheets.

VerfiX Trust Engine replaces this fragmentation with a unified risk intelligence layer that runs automatically on every verification, screens continuously against updated lists, generates a composite Trust Score, and applies configurable decision rules — all while maintaining a complete, immutable audit trail.

Core Capabilities

Capability Detail Configuration
AML Screening Real-time screening against 60+ global AML lists including SECO, UN Security Council, EU Consolidated List, OFAC SDN, HM Treasury, and others. Lists selectable. Match threshold configurable per risk profile.
PEP Screening Identification of politically exposed persons, their family members (first and second degree), and close associates. Multiple international PEP databases. PEP tiers configurable. EDD trigger on PEP match.
Sanctions Screening Continuous monitoring against government-maintained sanctions lists. Batch rescreening of existing customer base. Rescreening frequency configurable: daily, weekly, or event-triggered.
Risk Scoring Composite Trust Score 0–100 aggregating KYC result, AML result, document fraud signals, biometric confidence, device intelligence, and jurisdiction risk. Score weights configurable. Score thresholds for Approve / Review / Reject.
Fraud Detection Deepfake detection, synthetic identity signals, document fraud patterns, device fingerprinting, and behavioural anomaly detection. Individual fraud signals contribute to Trust Score.
Decision Intelligence Automated decision rules: Approve (score above threshold), Review (score in review band), or Reject (score below threshold or hard-stop trigger). Thresholds configurable per product and risk tier.
Webhooks Real-time event notifications for verification completed, AML hit detected, risk threshold breached, and ongoing monitoring alert. Webhook endpoints configurable per event type.
Ongoing Monitoring Scheduled or event-triggered rescreening of entire customer base against updated lists. Generates alert on new match. Frequency and scope configurable.

The Trust Score

⬡ Diagram: Trust Score Composition — Input signals feeding into a central scoring engine: (1) Document validity score, (2) Biometric match score, (3) Liveness score, (4) AML screening result (clear / hit / pending), (5) PEP status, (6) Sanctions status, (7) Fraud signals (deepfake / synthetic / document fraud), (8) Jurisdiction risk, (9) Device intelligence → Combined into Trust Score 0–100 → Decision Engine applies thresholds → Output: APPROVE / REVIEW / REJECT + Audit Trail

The Trust Score provides a single, interpretable risk indicator that allows compliance teams to:

  • ›Set a threshold above which verifications are automatically approved without manual review
  • ›Define a review band within which cases are routed to a compliance analyst
  • ›Set hard-stop triggers (for example: sanctions match = automatic reject regardless of score)
  • ›Maintain a documented, auditable decision process that satisfies FINMA and GwG requirements

Compliance Alignment

  • ›GwG Art. 5: Transaction monitoring support — Trust Engine ongoing monitoring designed to support GwG transaction monitoring obligations.
  • ›GwG Art. 6 para. 2: KYC refresh trigger — automatic alert when existing customer matches new list entry, triggering KYC refresh workflow.
  • ›GwG Art. 13: Enhanced due diligence — configurable EDD workflow triggered by PEP match, high-risk jurisdiction, or score below threshold.
  • ›FINMA AML guidance: Ongoing monitoring cadence and documentation aligned with FINMA supervisory expectations.
  • ›nFADP: All screening data processed in Switzerland. No personal data transferred outside Switzerland for screening.
