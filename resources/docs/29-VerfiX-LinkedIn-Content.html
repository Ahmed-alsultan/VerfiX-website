About Section (2,000 characters maximum)

VerfiX AG builds the trust layer for digital identity verification in Switzerland and Europe.

We provide regulated organisations with the infrastructure to verify who they are dealing with — individuals, businesses, and now EU Digital Identity Wallets — through a single, Swiss-hosted API.

Our platform comprises four products: KYC for individual identity verification, KYB for business verification and UBO identification, eID Gateway for accepting EU Digital Identity Wallet credentials, and Trust Engine for AML screening, risk scoring, and compliance decision intelligence.

All processing occurs in Switzerland. All data stays in Switzerland. FINMA Circular 2016/7, GwG, and nFADP compliance is built into the platform by design.

We serve banks, fintechs, crypto platforms, insurance companies, telecom operators, government agencies, universities, and regulated businesses across Switzerland and Europe.

One API. Full audit trail. Swiss-first.

Post Templates — Regulatory Updates

Post 1: FATF Grey Listing Context

Switzerland's FATF grey listing is a wake-up call for compliance teams.

FINMA has committed to increased supervisory intensity. AML programme quality that passed inspection in 2023 is now being scrutinised more rigorously.

What this means for Swiss financial institutions:

→ KYC documentation must be immediately producible for FINMA inspection

→ Ongoing AML monitoring must be evidenced, not just claimed

→ Beneficial owner identification gaps are a priority enforcement target

VerfiX was built for exactly this environment. Swiss-hosted. FINMA-aligned. Inspection-ready audit trail built in.

Post 2: nFADP and Biometric Data

Since 1 September 2023, biometric data is a sensitive personal data category under Swiss law (nFADP).

This matters for every Swiss organisation using facial recognition or biometric matching for KYC.

What it requires:

→ Documented legal basis for biometric processing

→ Written Data Processing Agreement with your KYC vendor

→ Swiss data residency (biometric data should not leave Switzerland)

→ Data minimisation: biometric templates deleted after the session by default

VerfiX processes all biometric data in Switzerland, provides a standard DPA, and does not retain biometric templates beyond the session by default.

Post 4: Thought Leadership

Swiss banks are preparing for eIDAS 2.0. Here is why it matters even though Switzerland is not an EU member.

The EU Digital Identity Wallet (EUDIW) will be available to all EU citizens by 2026. For Swiss banks with EU clients, this creates a choice: accept EUDIW credentials for onboarding, or create friction for EU customers who expect their digital ID to work.

The challenge: 27 EU countries, 27 different wallet implementations.

The solution: VerfiX eID Gateway. One integration. All 27 EU wallets. Standardised result.
