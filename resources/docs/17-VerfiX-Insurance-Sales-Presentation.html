Identity Infrastructure for Swiss Insurers

VerfiX for Insurance Companies

VerfiX provides Swiss insurance companies with identity verification for policyholder onboarding, claims identity confirmation, voice authentication for telephone services, and AML screening — all via a single Swiss-hosted API.

Insurance Identity Verification Requirements

Swiss insurance companies providing life insurance and investment-linked products are subject to GwG identification requirements. Beyond the regulatory minimum, insurance companies face identity-related challenges across the customer lifecycle:

  • ›Onboarding: Remote policyholder onboarding without in-person identification. Growing customer preference for fully digital insurance purchase.
  • ›Claims: Identity confirmation at the point of claims submission to prevent fraudulent claims from persons other than the policyholder.
  • ›Telephone services: Authentication for policyholder enquiries, mid-policy changes, and claims status calls without relying on vulnerable PIN-based authentication.
  • ›AML: Life insurance products are recognised AML risk vectors. GwG applies to most life insurance business. Ongoing AML screening is required.

Life Insurance AML Requirements

Life insurance products — particularly those with an investment component or significant cash value — are subject to GwG AML requirements. Insurance companies providing such products must:

  • ›Identify policyholders and beneficial owners (GwG Art. 3 & 4)
  • ›Screen against AML and PEP lists at onboarding
  • ›Maintain ongoing monitoring of existing policyholder base
  • ›Apply enhanced due diligence for PEP policyholders and high-risk structures
  • ›Document all AML activity in a form that can be produced for FINMA inspection

VerfiX Trust Engine addresses all of these requirements in a single integration. AML screening is conducted in real time at onboarding, and ongoing monitoring rescreens the policyholder base automatically.

Fraud Prevention in Claims

Identity-based claims fraud — where a person submits a claim impersonating the legitimate policyholder — is a material loss source for Swiss insurers. VerfiX biometric verification at the point of claim submission creates a strong deterrent:

⬡ Diagram: Claims Fraud Prevention — Claimant submits claim online → VerfiX KYC API called → Claimant completes document verification + selfie → Biometric match against policyholder record (stored at onboarding) → High match: claim proceeds → Low match or liveness failure: flagged for investigation → Audit trail generated

By comparing the biometric data captured at claim time against the biometric data captured at onboarding, VerfiX identifies cases where the person making the claim does not match the policyholder — without relying on the claimant to know policy-specific knowledge questions.
