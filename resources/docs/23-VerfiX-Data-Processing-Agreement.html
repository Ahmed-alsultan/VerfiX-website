Data Processing Agreement — VerfiX AG

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") is entered into between VerfiX AG, a Swiss company ("Processor"), and the Customer organisation identified in the associated Service Agreement ("Controller").

This DPA forms part of the Service Agreement between the Processor and the Controller and governs the processing of personal data by the Processor on behalf of the Controller in connection with the VerfiX identity verification platform ("Services").

Note: This DPA is provided as a template for legal review. It should be reviewed and approved by qualified legal counsel before execution.

1. Subject Matter and Duration

The Processor processes personal data on behalf of the Controller for the purpose of providing the Services described in the Service Agreement. This DPA is effective from the date the Controller first accesses the Services and remains in force for the duration of the Service Agreement. Data retention and deletion obligations survive termination as specified in Section 9.

2. Nature and Purpose of Processing

The Processor processes personal data to provide the Services, which include identity verification, business verification, AML screening, risk scoring, and related compliance functions. Processing occurs only on documented instructions from the Controller, except where required by Swiss law.

3. Categories of Data Subjects

The personal data processed under this DPA relates to:

  • ›Individuals whose identity the Controller verifies through the Services (new customers, existing customers undergoing refresh, beneficial owners, company directors)
  • ›Authorised user accounts of the Controller who access the VerfiX dashboard and API

4. Types of Personal Data

Data type Processing context
Full name, date of birth, nationality Document verification and AML screening
Document number, expiry date, issuing country Document verification
Facial biometric data (image, template) Face verification — ephemeral processing, not retained by default
Voice biometric data Voice verification — ephemeral processing, not retained by default
NFC chip data ePassport NFC verification — ephemeral processing
AML screening results (including match data) Sanctions, PEP, and adverse media screening — retained as part of compliance record
Risk score and decision outcome Trust Engine — retained as part of compliance record

5. Processor Obligations

  • ›Process personal data only on documented instructions from the Controller, unless required to do otherwise by Swiss law.
  • ›Ensure that persons authorised to process personal data are bound by confidentiality obligations.
  • ›Implement appropriate technical and organisational measures as described in Section 6.
  • ›Engage sub-processors only in accordance with Section 7.
  • ›Assist the Controller in fulfilling data subject rights requests, at the Controller's expense.
  • ›Assist the Controller in fulfilling obligations under nFADP Articles 7, 22, and 24 (privacy by design, impact assessment, breach notification).
  • ›Delete or return all personal data to the Controller upon termination of the Service Agreement, as specified in Section 9.
  • ›Provide all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA.
  • ›Inform the Controller immediately if, in the Processor's opinion, an instruction infringes applicable data protection law.

6. Technical and Organisational Measures

The Processor applies the following technical and organisational measures to protect personal data:

  • ›Encryption: AES-256 at rest; TLS 1.3 in transit
  • ›Access control: Role-based access control; multi-factor authentication for administrative access
  • ›Data residency: All processing and storage in Switzerland
  • ›Audit logging: All data access and processing events logged in immutable audit trail
  • ›Incident response: 72-hour notification to Controller of confirmed breach likely to result in risk to data subjects
  • ›Staff: All personnel with access to personal data bound by confidentiality obligations

7. Sub-processors

The Processor engages a limited number of sub-processors as listed at verfix.ch/trust-center. The Processor will notify the Controller at least 30 days before engaging a new sub-processor or materially changing the role of an existing sub-processor. The Controller may object within this period. If the Processor proceeds over the Controller's objection, the Controller may terminate the Service Agreement.

All sub-processors are required to apply data protection measures at least equivalent to those described in this DPA.

8. Cross-Border Data Transfers

The Processor processes all personal data exclusively within Switzerland. No personal data is transferred outside Switzerland without the Controller's prior written consent, except as required by Swiss law. Where the Processor uses sub-processors located outside Switzerland, the Processor will ensure that appropriate safeguards are in place in accordance with nFADP.

9. Retention and Deletion

Upon termination of the Service Agreement, or at the Controller's written request, the Processor will, within 30 days, either delete or return all personal data processed under this DPA, and certify in writing that deletion has occurred. The Processor may retain personal data to the extent required by Swiss law, in which case it will inform the Controller of the applicable legal obligation and the retention period.

For biometric data (facial photographs, biometric templates): deleted at end of session by default. The Controller may configure longer retention where legally justified.

For compliance records (verification outcomes, AML results, audit trail): retained for 10 years by default (GwG Art. 8) unless the Controller specifies a shorter period.

10. Audit Rights

The Controller may audit the Processor's compliance with this DPA up to once per calendar year, upon at least 30 days' prior written notice, at the Controller's expense. The Processor will provide all information reasonably necessary to demonstrate compliance. The Controller may appoint a qualified independent auditor; the auditor must be subject to appropriate confidentiality obligations.

11. Data Subject Rights

The Processor will assist the Controller in responding to data subject rights requests under nFADP (access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability). Where a data subject makes a request directly to the Processor, the Processor will forward it to the Controller within 5 business days.

12. Breach Notification

The Processor will notify the Controller within 72 hours of becoming aware of a personal data breach likely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of data subjects. The notification will include the nature of the breach, the categories and approximate number of affected data subjects, the likely consequences, and the measures taken or proposed by the Processor. The Controller is responsible for notifying the FDPIC where required.

13. Governing Law

This DPA is governed by Swiss law. Any disputes arising from this DPA shall be subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of the Canton of Vaud, Switzerland.
