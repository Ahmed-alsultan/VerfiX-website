Information Security Incident Response

Incident Response Policy — VerfiX AG

This policy defines VerfiX AG's approach to detecting, responding to, containing, and recovering from information security incidents, including personal data breaches. It applies to all VerfiX AG systems, personnel, and third-party providers with access to VerfiX infrastructure or customer data.

1. Scope

This policy applies to all incidents involving:

  • Unauthorised access to or disclosure of customer personal data
  • Unauthorised access to VerfiX production systems or infrastructure
  • Loss or destruction of customer data
  • Service disruption affecting customer availability (DDoS, ransomware, system failure)
  • Compromise of API keys or authentication credentials
  • Suspected insider threat or unauthorised internal access to customer data

2. Incident Severity Classification

Severity Criteria Initial response SLA
Critical Personal data breach affecting multiple customers. Service outage affecting >10% of customers. Confirmed unauthorised access to production systems. Response within 1 hour. Executive notification within 2 hours.
High Personal data breach affecting a single customer. Suspected (unconfirmed) unauthorised access. Significant service degradation. Response within 4 hours. Management notification within 6 hours.
Medium Security policy violation without confirmed data access. Service degradation below SLA threshold. Suspected credential compromise. Response within 24 hours. Management notification within 48 hours.
Low Policy violation without security impact. Near-miss or attempted attack that was successfully blocked. Response within 72 hours. Documented for trend analysis.

3. Incident Response Phases

Phase 1: Detection and Identification

Incidents may be detected through automated monitoring alerts, employee reports, customer reports, or third-party notifications. All potential incidents must be reported to security@verfix.ch immediately upon detection.

The security team will perform an initial assessment to: confirm the incident is genuine (not a false positive), classify the severity, identify the scope of systems and data affected, and initiate the appropriate response track.

Phase 2: Containment

Containment measures must be implemented before any remediation or forensic investigation. Containment actions may include:

  • Revocation of compromised API keys or credentials
  • Network isolation of affected systems
  • Blocking of identified attack vectors (IP addresses, malicious requests)
  • Suspension of affected services where necessary to prevent further damage

Phase 3: Assessment and Evidence Preservation

Following initial containment, the security team will:

  • Determine the nature, scope, and root cause of the incident
  • Identify all customer data affected, including data categories and approximate volume
  • Preserve forensic evidence: system logs, access logs, network captures, audit trail records
  • Assess the risk to affected data subjects

Phase 4: Customer and Authority Notification

Customer notification commitments:

  • Critical and High severity incidents: Affected customers notified within 72 hours of confirmed breach, in accordance with nFADP Art. 24 and DPA commitments
  • Notification content: Nature of the breach, data affected, likely consequences, measures taken and proposed
  • Updates: Subsequent updates provided at minimum every 24 hours during an active Critical or High incident

Regulatory notification:

  • VerfiX will advise affected customers on the requirement to notify the FDPIC (Federal Data Protection and Information Commissioner) where required by nFADP
  • VerfiX will cooperate with regulatory investigations and provide requested information within the statutory timeframe

Phase 5: Remediation and Recovery

Once the incident is contained and evidence is preserved, the security team will implement remediation measures to address the root cause and restore normal service. Remediation may include:

  • Patching or updating affected systems
  • Strengthening access controls or authentication
  • Updating firewall rules or network segmentation
  • Replacing compromised credentials across all affected systems

Phase 6: Post-Incident Review

A written post-incident report will be produced within 30 days of incident resolution, covering: timeline, root cause, impact, response effectiveness, and remediation actions. This report will be:

  • Provided to affected customers on request
  • Used internally for security programme improvement
  • Retained for a minimum of 5 years

4. Customer Communication Template

The following is the template for initial customer notification of a personal data breach:

Subject: VerfiX Security Notice — [Incident Reference] — Action Required

Dear [Customer contact name],

We are writing to inform you of a security incident affecting VerfiX AG that may involve personal data processed on your behalf. We take the security of your data extremely seriously and are writing to you as soon as we have confirmed the nature and scope of the incident.

[Description of incident: what occurred, when, how detected]

[Description of data affected: categories, approximate data subjects]

[Actions VerfiX has taken: containment, remediation steps]

[Actions you may need to take: assessment of notification obligations, contacting affected data subjects]

[Contact for further information: security@verfix.ch]
