Video Identification

VerfiX Video Identification enables fully automated digital customer identification via video, compliant with FINMA Circular 2016/7. The system checks identity documents for authenticity, reads all relevant data via OCR, and biometrically compares the document photo with the user's selfie. Liveness detection protects against deepfakes and spoofing attacks.

Everything about VerfiX Video Identification — process, regulation and integration.

Video Identification follows a guided, fully digital 4-step process:

  • Document capture: The user films their identity document (passport, ID card, residence permit) from both sides. The system verifies authenticity and readability in real time.
  • OCR extraction: All relevant fields are automatically read and validated against reference data.
  • Biometric matching: The user takes a selfie or short video. The system compares the document photo with the current face.
  • Liveness check: Detects a living person — protecting against photos, masks and deepfakes.

The full process typically takes under 5 minutes and is fully mobile-accessible.

Yes. FINMA Circular 2016/7 ("Video and Online Identification") explicitly permits customer identification via video as an equivalent alternative to in-person identification at a branch.

VerfiX satisfies all technical requirements of the circular: real-time document verification with security feature recognition, biometric face matching with documented confidence score, liveness detection, and full process documentation and archiving.

A fully automated Video ID process takes 2–5 minutes. On average, users need approximately 3.5 minutes — provided the identity document is clearly legible and lighting conditions are adequate.

VerfiX supports over 4,500 identity document types from more than 190 countries: passports (all ICAO-compliant countries), national identity cards (EU, Switzerland, EEA), residence permits, and driving licences (selected countries). The document database is continuously updated.

All data captured during Video Identification is stored exclusively in Switzerland. VerfiX offers three deployment options: Swiss Cloud, Swiss Dedicated (isolated infrastructure), and On-Premise within your own Swiss infrastructure.

All failures are documented in the audit log including reason, timestamp, and confidence score.
